What I notice

I identify the moments when technical possibility starts becoming social infrastructure — where assumptions, incentives, relationships, and power begin to shape the future.

Principles

Principles for work that earns trust.

These commitments guide how I approach technology, strategy, collaboration, and the relationships and long-term impacts shaped by AI-enabled systems.

Begin with relationship

I look beyond users, use cases, and organizational priorities to understand the people, communities, institutions, and ecosystems affected by the work.

Make judgment visible

I name assumptions, tradeoffs, risks, and open questions so decisions can be examined before they become infrastructure.

Design for stewardship

I treat launch as the beginning of responsibility, not the end of design — planning for care, governance, adaptation, and accountability.

Relational Accountability / Human Agency / Long-Term Responsibility

From emerging questions to responsible direction.

My process creates space to examine what is being built, who is affected, what could go wrong, and what care and accountability require us to do next.

1

Frame the responsibility

Clarify the context, who is affected, intended outcomes, constraints, and values that should guide the work.

2

Explore the tensions

Surface assumptions, ethical questions, adoption barriers, and the choices that shape different futures.

3

Shape the system

Translate insight into frameworks, prototypes, narratives, policies, or practices that can be tested, adapted, and cared for over time.
